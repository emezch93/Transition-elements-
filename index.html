Why are Scandium and Zinc Not Colored?

Scandium (Sc): In its +3 oxidation state (Sc³⁺), all 3d electrons are lost, leaving an empty d-orbital. Since d–d transitions require partially filled d-orbitals, no visible light absorption occurs, making Scandium compounds colorless.

Zinc (Zn): In its +2 oxidation state (Zn²⁺), the 3d orbital is completely filled (3d¹⁰), meaning no electron transitions can occur. As a result, Zinc compounds are also colorless.

**Colored transition metals** have partially filled d-orbitals, allowing d–d transitions that absorb specific wavelengths of visible light, making them appear colored.
